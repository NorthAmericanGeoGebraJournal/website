# North American GeoGebra Journal

The **North American GeoGebra Journal (NAGJ)** is a peer-reviewed,
open-access academic publication dedicated to the innovative use of
GeoGebra in mathematics education.

The journal publishes articles that explore the integration of GeoGebra
in teaching, learning, curriculum development, and research across
grades K–16 (kindergarten through undergraduate education).

## Mission

The mission of NAGJ is to provide educators and researchers with a
venue for sharing high-quality work involving dynamic mathematics,
technology-enhanced learning, and innovative approaches to mathematics
education.

## Scope

Topics of interest include:

- GeoGebra applications in mathematics classrooms
- Dynamic geometry and visualization
- Technology-enhanced mathematics instruction
- Teacher education and professional development
- Curriculum design and assessment
- Research on technology-supported learning

## Publication Information

- **Publication model:** Open access
- **Review process:** Double-blind peer review
- **Submission fees:** None
- **Publication fees:** None

The journal continues the tradition of the original GeoGebra journal
collection and is transitioning to a modern GitHub-based publishing
workflow.
