abstract: |
  Surfaces can be graphed in GeoGebra by either using the Surface command,
  defining a function f (x, y), or inputting an equation of at most three
  variables. Some surfaces can be easily graphed using a function of two
  variables or an equation, but we have more control over the surface if the
  Surface command is used. We start by graphing surfaces of revolution
  encountered in single variable calculus class. A surface of revolution can
  be graphed in GeoGebra provided we can parameterize the circular
  cross-section. This method of graphing a surface of revolution can be
  applied to model objects with circular cross-section, like a Hershey's Kiss,
  in GeoGebra.
