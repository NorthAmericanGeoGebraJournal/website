abstract: |
  Simulations of events, using Monte Carlo methods in Geogebra, provide
  students with opportunities to actively participate in statistical events.
  Simulations can be used to derive statistical information about underlying
  distributions. In the following paper, we explore dart throwing and needle
  drop simulations as vehicles for estimating. Connecting abstract concepts to
  visually compelling media provides teachers with innovative approaches for
  teaching probability.
