abstract: |
  The Fourier transform (and all its versions,
  discrete/continuous/finite/infinite), covers deep and abstract mathematical
  concepts, and can easily overwhelm with detail. In this paper I provide some
  intuitive ideas of how the discrete Fourier transform (and its version with
  low frequencies) works and how we can use it to approximate real periodic
  functions and parametric closed curves by means of trigonometric
  interpolation.
