abstract: |
  This article explores a problem solving strategy called "messing around"
  that is particularly apt for online course work in GeoGebra. While
  traditional math problems may require students to search deliberately for a
  specific solution, messing around employs a more fortuitous approach. We
  dissect this approach and examine multiple GeoGebra apps that support
  messing around by providing students with a sandbox for mathematical
  experimentation.
