abstract: |
  In this document we describe the necessary constructions for modeling the
  apparent position of the Sun in the sky in GeoGebra. We discuss how to build
  a planet that moves and rotates about its axis and a way to position the
  observer at a given latitude from which we will compute the horizontal
  coordinates of the Sun. Later these computed coordinates are represented in
  a celestial sphere where the horizon plane for the observer is fixed, giving
  the apparent track of the Sun at different days of the year.
