  The author illustrates a method for motivating mathematical generalization
  with students using GeoGebra. Building on student interest in amusement
  parks, the author connects an initial question about Ferris wheel riders to
  general quadrilaterals in the plane. In a culminating activity, students
  construct a rigorous algebraic proof confirming their initial conjectures.
