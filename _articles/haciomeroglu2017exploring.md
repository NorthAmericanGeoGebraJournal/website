abstract: |
  Technology has changed the nature of mathematics learning and instructional
  practices (Andreasen and Haciomeroglu, 2013; Edwards, 2015; Haciomeroglu,
  Bu, Schoen, and Hohenwarter, 2011). Dynamic and interactive technology
  enriches students' learning opportunities and shifts the focus of
  instruction to understanding and student-centered learning by providing a
  means of modeling mathematical relationships (Bu and Henson, 2016;
  Haciomeroglu, Bu, Schoen, and Hohenwarter, 2011). The authors connect
  Euclidean and non-Euclidean geometries through an exploration of rich tasks
  of Taxicab geometry, sharing methods for organizing and presenting tasks to
  enhance students' understanding of geometry concepts.
