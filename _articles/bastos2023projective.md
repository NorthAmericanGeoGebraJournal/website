abstract: |
  This article aims to bring a discussion focused on the historical evolution
  of projective geometry and perspectives that aim at its application in the
  educational context, especially with the use of GeoGebra for the
  presentation of its concepts. The historical bias of this geometry field was
  analyzed in the light of the possibilities of its approach in basic
  education. The research methodology of this work is of a qualitative nature,
  bibliographic in nature, where we relate materials that bring the evolution
  of projective geometry from its genesis to the present day. In the meantime,
  we bring a perspective of transition between the Euclidean-projective
  geometries, as a way to elucidate some concepts of projective geometry and
  facilitate its understanding from the visualization with the contribution of
  GeoGebra.
